# 1.0 Seasonal Adjustments (SA)

## Overview
Treasury Inflation-Protected Securities (TIPS) yields are calculated based on the "**[Clean Price](../../knowledge/DATA_DICTIONARY.md#clean-price)**", which is Canty's term for the unadjusted price; this price does not include the inflation adjustment component. However, since TIPS are indexed to the Non-Seasonally Adjusted CPI (CPI-NSA), their monthly performance is subject to predictable seasonal patterns (e.g., higher inflation in spring, lower in autumn).

The **[Seasonal Adjustment (SA)](../../knowledge/DATA_DICTIONARY.md#sa-factor)** adjusts these yields by applying SA factors, providing a more accurate comparison of real yields across different months of the year by adjusting the yields to be consistent with institutional traders' views of seasonal impacts on nominal returns.

---

## Explaining Seasonality

### Level 1: Common Sense
Prices for things like gas, clothing, and food go up and down with the seasons (e.g., higher demand for gas in summer). This "noise" makes it look like inflation is moving faster or slower than it really is. We "fix" the math by smoothing out these seasonal waves so you can see the real value of the TIPS without being fooled by the time of year.

### Level 2: The Boglehead Perspective
Evidence-based investing requires accurate data. A raw TIPS yield in April might look higher than one in October simply because of historical inflation patterns, not because the bond is actually a better deal. Seasonal adjustment allows for a "fair" comparison across the entire yield curve by removing predictable, recurring CPI-NSA fluctuations.

### Level 3: Institutional Logic
Institutional TIPS traders (the ones who set the market prices) don't trade based on raw NSA yields. They look at "Seasonally Adjusted" yields to identify "cheap" or "rich" spots on the curve. By applying these same factors, we are viewing the market through the same lens as the professionals.

### Level 4: The Math
We apply multiplicative seasonal factors derived from the BLS CPI-U series (Comparing the Non-Seasonally Adjusted index to the Seasonally Adjusted index).
- **Formula**: $SA\_Factor = \frac{Index_{NSA}}{Index_{SA}}$
- **Yield Impact**: We adjust the TIPS price by the ratio of the settlement factor to the maturity factor, then re-calculate the Internal Rate of Return (IRR).

---

## Terminology
- **[SA (Seasonal Adjustment)](../../knowledge/DATA_DICTIONARY.md#sa-factor)**: The multiplicative factor used to normalize for seasonal inflation patterns.
- **[SA Yield (Seasonally Adjusted Yield)](../../knowledge/DATA_DICTIONARY.md#sa-yield)**: The real yield derived from a Seasonally Adjusted Price.

## The Transformation
The transformation converts a **Market Price** to a **Seasonally Adjusted Price**, which is then used to derive the **SA Yield**.

### 1. Price Adjustment Formula
\[ P_{SA} = P_{Price} \times \frac{S_{settle}}{S_{maturity}} \]

Where:
- \( P_{SA} \): Seasonally Adjusted Price.
- \( P_{Price} \): Market Price.
- \( S_{settle} \): SA Factor for the **[Settlement Date](../../knowledge/DATA_DICTIONARY.md#settlement-date)** (\( \frac{RefCPI_{NSA}}{RefCPI_{SA}} \)).
- \( S_{maturity} \): SA Factor for the **[Maturity Date](../../knowledge/DATA_DICTIONARY.md#maturity-date)** (\( \frac{RefCPI_{NSA}}{RefCPI_{SA}} \)).

Note: The SA Factor (\( S \)) is the ratio of the **[Reference CPI](../../knowledge/DATA_DICTIONARY.md#ref-cpi)** (Non-Seasonally Adjusted) to the Reference CPI (Seasonally Adjusted) for the specific date. This formula is generic with respect to the type of price. Currently, the system uses FedInvest and broker ask prices, but it is not locked to "ask" prices only.

### 2. Yield Derivation
The **SA Yield** is derived from \( P_{SA} \) using the standard bond **[Yield-to-Maturity (YTM)](../../knowledge/DATA_DICTIONARY.md#yield)** formula (Actual/Actual day count convention, semi-annual compounding).

## Data Sources
- **Prices**: Sourced from FedInvest (daily) or uploaded via Broker CSV (Schwab/Fidelity). Currently, these sources provide **Ask Prices**.
- **SA Factors**: Calculated monthly from BLS CPI-U data (NSA vs SA components) and hosted in the Treasury Investors Portal R2 bucket.

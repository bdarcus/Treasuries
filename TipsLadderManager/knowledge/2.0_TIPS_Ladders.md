## TIPS Ladder Terminology

**[DARA (Desired Annual Real Amount)](../../knowledge/DATA_DICTIONARY.md#dara):** Target total cash flow for a funded year in real (inflation-adjusted) terms
- Replaces DAA from 2.0 Bond Ladders
- "Real" means in today's dollars

**[ARA (Annual Real Amount)](../../knowledge/DATA_DICTIONARY.md#ara):** Actual cash flow produced by the ladder for a funded year in real terms
- Replaces AA from 2.0 Bond Ladders
- May differ from DARA due to rounding

**Note on "Real":** All principal and interest values are inflation-adjusted, so "real" is implied throughout

## Gap Years

**[Gap Years](../../knowledge/DATA_DICTIONARY.md#gap-years):** Years within the ladder period where no TIPS exist (currently 2037, 2038, 2039).

**Synthetic TIPS for Gap Years:**
Created to maintain ladder continuity and facilitate duration matching.
- **Maturity**: January 15 of the gap year (10-year TIPS are issued in January and July).
- **Index ratio**: 1.0 (par assumption).
- **Price**: 100 (par).

**Gap Parameter Calculation:**
Synthetic TIPS are processed **longest to shortest** to model a hypothetical ladder as if gap TIPS existed. This establishes the **Gap Total Cost** (used for bracket sizing) and **Gap Avg Duration** (used for duration matching).

**LMI for gap years** uses the same definition as for any other rung: annual coupon interest from ALL TIPS maturing after the gap year. This includes:
- **Actual TIPS LMI**: annual interest from funded years above the gap year. When `preLadderInterest` is active, only funded years with non-zero TIPS purchased contribute (zeroed funded years have no actual TIPS and therefore no coupon income).
- **Synthetic LMI** (`runningSynLMI`): accumulated synthetic interest from longer gap years already processed in this sweep (same accumulator pattern as the main ladder).

**Pre-Ladder Interest (PLI) — single interleaved pass** *(Build and Rebalance, when `preLadderInterest` enabled)*:
The PLI pool is distributed across ALL years in maturity order (short→long), with funded years and gap years interleaved. Gap years consume pool before longer-dated funded years (e.g., 2037–2039 are processed before 2040), so whether a funded year like 2040 gets zeroed depends on how much pool the gap years consumed first.
```
remaining = preLadderPool
for year in allYears ascending (short→long):   // allYears = rangeYears ∪ gapYears, sorted
  if year is a funded year:
    need = MAX(0, DARA - prelim[year].laterMatInt)
    if remaining >= need:
      zeroedFundedYears.add(year)
      remaining -= need
    else:
      partialCreditYear = year; partialCredit = remaining; remaining = 0; break
  if year is a gap year:
    need = MAX(0, DARA - actualTIPSLMI(year))   // full prelim LMI for all funded years above; synth LMI not yet known
    if remaining >= need:
      pliCredit[year] = need
      remaining -= need
    else:
      pliCredit[year] = remaining
      remaining = 0; break
```
After this pass, `zeroedFundedYears` is fully determined (including whether bracket years like 2040 are zeroed).

**Steps** (long→short qty sweep):
1. **Anchors**: `anchorBefore` (latest Jan TIPS before gap) and `anchorAfter` (earliest Feb TIPS after gap).
2. **Interpolate Yield**: Linear interpolation between anchors using maturity dates.
3. **Synthetic Coupon**: `MAX(0.00125, FLOOR(yield * 100 / 0.125) * 0.00125)`.
4. **Duration**: Calculate Modified Duration for each synthetic TIPS.
5. **PI per Synthetic TIPS**: `PI_y = 1,000 × (1 + coupon / 2)` (nPeriods = 1, Jan maturity; in dollars).
6. **Required Quantity**: `qty_y = MAX(0, ROUND((DARA − runningSynLMI − actualTIPSLMI − pliCredit[y]) / PI_y))`.
   - `runningSynLMI` = accumulated synthetic interest from longer gap years already processed in this sweep.
   - `actualTIPSLMI` = sum of annual coupon interest from all actual funded years above this gap year.
   - `pliCredit[y]` = PLI pool credit allocated to this gap year (0 when `preLadderInterest` is disabled).
   - Synthetic TIPS in ladder 1 are constructed exactly like any other rung: LMI and PLI credit reduce the required quantity the same way they do for actual funded years.
7. **LMI Update**: `runningSynLMI += qty_y × 1,000 × coupon`. This accumulated synthetic interest **does** feed into the `runningSynLMI` term for shorter gap years in subsequent iterations.

## Gap Year Coverage Model

The gap coverage algorithm can be understood as two equivalent constructions:

**Component 1 — Synthetic ladder:**
Every year in the ladder period — funded years and gap years alike — is treated as a rung. Gap years receive synthetic TIPS (hypothetical, never purchased) sized so each gap year delivers ~DARA, exactly like any funded year. Each synthetic TIPS generates annual coupon that flows as LMI to shorter-maturity rungs, including the funded year immediately below the gap block. No excess exists anywhere in this view.

**Component 2 — Bracket excess:**
Same construction, but gap years have no TIPS. The theoretical cost of the synthetic gap TIPS is instead allocated to bracket excess holdings at the lower and upper bracket years, split by duration weight. The excess bonds' annual coupon approximately replaces the LMI that the synthetic gap TIPS would have provided:
- **Upper bracket (2040) excess coupon** flows as LMI to 2036 (and all shorter rungs).
- **Lower bracket (2036) excess coupon** flows as LMI to the 2036 funded year itself, and to shorter rungs.

The replacement is approximate because synthetic gap TIPS (par, coupon from yield interpolation) have different coupon rates than the actual bracket TIPS. This causes a small residual.

**Bracket year ARA vs DARA:**
In the synthetic view, 2036 is a plain funded year sized for `ARA ≈ DARA`. In the actual ladder, the 2036 excess coupon also flows to the 2036 funded year as additional LMI, slightly reducing the funded-year quantity needed. The net effect is that the bracket year's total `ARA = DARA + small delta`, where the delta arises from the coupon rate difference between synthetic and actual TIPS. This is expected behavior, not a bug.

**LMI is always annual coupon interest only — never principal.** P+I (principal + last-year interest received at maturity) is a separate concept applied only to the funded year that holds the TIPS. Excess bracket bonds never contribute their principal to another rung's LMI.

**Excess coupon always flows inward:** Excess bracket coupon always contributes to LMI for shorter-maturity rungs within the ladder. The shortest rung in the ladder has no shorter rungs to receive the flow — the coupon is applied where shorter rungs exist, and there is no "exit" from the ladder.

## Future 30Y Rungs

**Future 30Y Rungs:** Years beyond the last-issued TIPS maturity year (currently 2056). Future 30Y rungs arise when `lastYear > 2056` (max `lastYear` = 2066).

**Hypothetical TIPS for Future 30Y Rungs:**
Processed **longest to shortest** using the longest-dated actual TIPS (currently 2056) as a flat-curve anchor:
- **Anchor**: Coupon and yield of the 2056 TIPS.
- **Maturity**: February 15 of the Future 30Y year (30-year TIPS are issued in February).
- **Index ratio / Price**: 1.0 / 100 (par assumptions).
- **Duration**: Calculate Modified Duration for each hypothetical bond.
- **Quantity and Cost**: Calculated identically to Gap Years (see above) using the flat-curve anchor.

## Duration Matching (Brackets)

Missing blocks (Gap or Future 30Y) are covered by additional holdings in a **bracket pair** (or **cover pair**). The goal is for the excess holdings to change in value identically to the missing block if rates move.

### 2-Bracket Weights (Standard)
```
lowerWeight * d_lower + upperWeight * d_upper = averageBlockDuration
upperWeight = (averageBlockDuration - d_lower) / (d_upper - d_lower)
lowerWeight = 1 - upperWeight
```

### 3-Bracket Weights (Optional)
Used when an "original lower" bracket exists and we want to supplement it with a "new lower" and "upper" bracket without selling the original.
```
w1     = currentExcessOrigLower_cost / totalBlockCost   ← fixed (original lower)
w2_raw = (averageBlockDuration − d_upper + w1×(d_upper − d_origLower)) / (d_newLower − d_upper)
w2     = max(0, w2_raw)   ← clamp: if orig lower alone overshoots, skip new lower
w3     = 1 − w1 − w2
```

### Future 30Y Cover Pair
When `lastYear > 2056`, two existing TIPS are used to duration-match the hypothetical Future 30Y rungs:
- **Future 30Y lower cover**: Always the 2056 TIPS.
- **Future 30Y upper cover**: The TIPS with the longest modified duration excluding 2056 (currently 2052).

#### LMI at the 2056/Future 30Y Boundary

The 2056 funded year's LMI will appear low — and lower than the LMI shown for the 2057 hypothetical rung — for two structural reasons that are correct, not bugs:

1. **No actual bonds exist above 2056.** Future 30Y rungs (2057–2066) are hypothetical; their coupon income is synthetic, used only to size hypothetical quantities. None of it flows into the actual ladder's `runningLMI` pool.

2. **Duration constraint forces weight into 2052.** The hypothetical future30y TIPS are long-dated (~30–40 year modified duration from settlement). The 2056 TIPS has a higher coupon and therefore shorter duration than needed to match the average. `bracketWeights` must assign substantial weight to the 2052 TIPS (near-zero coupon, acts as a near-zero-coupon bond, long duration) to reach the target. The 2052 excess bonds mature before 2056 and their coupons flow only to years ≤ 2051 — they contribute nothing to 2056's LMI. Only the 2056 excess bonds (the lower-weight fraction) contribute coupon income to 2056.

The apparent inversion — `LMI(2057 hypothetical) > LMI(2056 actual)` — is therefore an apples-vs-oranges comparison: the 2057 figure is synthetic LMI from within `calcFuture30yParams`, while the 2056 figure is actual income from purchased bonds. The 2056 funded year is correctly sized as long as `ARA(2056) ≈ DARA`.

### Excess Quantities
For each bracket `b`:
```
excessQty_b = ROUND(totalBlockCost × weight_b / costPerBond_b)
```
where `costPerBond_b = price/100 × indexRatio × 1000` (per **TIPS Basics** — IR always included). This formula applies equally to `excessQtyBefore` and `excessQtyAfter` in Rebalance.

## Algorithm (Longest-to-Shortest Sweep)

Process actual bonds and funded years **longest to shortest**.

1. **Initialize LMI Pool**: `runningLMI = 0`.
2. **For each year Y (longest to shortest)**:
   a. **Incoming LMI**: Sum of inflation-adjusted `annualInterest` from all bonds maturing *after* Y.
   b. **Bracket Excess Contribution**: If year Y contains bracket excess bonds, their coupon interest contributes to the pool for year Y itself.
      - `excessLMI_Y = excessQty_Y × annualInterestPerBond_Y`
   c. **Target FY Quantity**:
      - `Required_PI = YearDARA - (IncomingLMI + excessLMI_Y)`
      - `fundedYearQty_Y = MAX(0, ROUND(Required_PI / piPerBond_Y))`
   d. **Update LMI Pool**:
      - `runningLMI += (fundedYearQty_Y + excessQty_Y) × annualInterestPerBond_Y`
3. **Gap Years**: Contribute zero to the `runningLMI` pool (no actual bonds held; their role is played by bracket excess in other years).

## Transparency & UI Requirements

Duration matching must be educational and traceable. The UI must provide:
1. **Individual Disclosure**: Every synthetic/hypothetical bond used to calculate the average must be listed with its own Modified Duration.
2. **Derivation**: The "Average Duration" must be explicitly shown as the mean of the listed bonds.
3. **Fulcrum Visualization**: A visual representation showing the bracket durations flanking the average duration.
4. **Cost-to-Quantity Chain**: A breakdown showing how the block's total cost is weighted and then converted to discrete bond units.

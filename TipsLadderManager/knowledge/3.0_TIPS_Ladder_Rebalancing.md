## Purpose

Rebalance an existing TIPS ladder to align with a desired amount target (DARA). Two modes:
- **Gap**: Minimal trades; sells excess bracket TIPS to fund newly available maturities (formerly gaps).
- **Full**: Rebuilds the entire ladder from the current portfolio's total cash value, resetting to a clean DARA target.

## Context

TIPS ladders require periodic rebalancing as new TIPS are issued and gaps are filled. Originally, gaps (years with no TIPS) were covered by additional TIPS in bracket years. As new TIPS are issued, rebalancing sells this additional amount to buy the new maturities. Additionally, market price changes may drift the ladder's income profile; a full rebalance resets the entire ladder to a clean DARA target using the current portfolio value.

## Rebalancing-Specific Concepts

### Official Ladder Range

- **firstYear**: Earliest year with holdings
- **lastYear**: Latest contiguous year with holdings before any missing rung above 2040.
- Missing rungs above 2040 truncate the official range (e.g., holdings in 2047 and 2049 but not 2048 â†’ lastYear = 2047)
- Known gap years (2037-2039, no TIPS issued) do NOT break contiguity if 2040 is held â€” **mechanism**: if 2040 is held, the range unconditionally extends through the gap years.
- **Non-contiguous TIPS > 2040**: Contribute annual interest to earlier years but are NOT counted as rungs for DARA calculation or ladder range determination unless `lastYearOverride` extends the range to include them.
- **`lastYearOverride`** (optional UI parameter): When provided, replaces the derived `lastYear`. Allows the user to declare the intended last funded year, which may be a gap year (e.g., 2039) or an extended year (e.g., 2042). The UI infers a sensible default and shows a dropdown for manual adjustment.
  - **Shortening**: When `lastYearOverride < derivedLastYear`, all holdings in years `(lastYearOverride, derivedLastYear]` that were contiguous with the original ladder are sold in full (produce sell rows in output). These years are excluded from the rebuild.
  - **Extending**: When `lastYearOverride > derivedLastYear`, years in `(derivedLastYear, lastYearOverride]` with no current holdings are treated as rebalance years â€” the appropriate TIPS for those years are identified from market data and buy rows are generated.
- **`firstYearOverride`** (optional UI parameter): When provided, replaces the derived `firstYear` (earliest year with holdings). Holdings in years `[derivedFirstYear, firstYearOverride)` are sold in full (produce sell rows in output). These years are excluded from the rebuild.

### lastYear as a Gap Year

When `lastYearOverride` is a gap year (i.e., `lastYear âˆˆ gapYears`), the upper bracket year (2040) is **not a funded rung** â€” all its holdings are treated as bracket excess:

- `fundedYearQty(2040) = 0` in Phase 3 and Phase 4 (`yearDara(2040) = 0`)
- `excessQtyBefore(2040)` follows the standard cost formula (`ROUND(totalBlockCost Ã— weight_2040 / costPerBond_2040)`). Since `fyQtyAfter(2040) = 0`, `fyQtyBefore = qtyBefore âˆ’ excessQtyBefore` approaches zero when the portfolio was correctly built.
- Phase 2 (`calculateGapParameters`): 2040's LMI contribution uses **actual current holdings qty** (not a target computed from DARA) since no funded-year target exists for 2040.
- `rungCount` = `lastYear âˆ’ firstYear + 1` (gap years count as rungs; their income is funded by bracket excess)

**UI inference for default lastYear**: After loading holdings, if the last maturity is 2040, `lastYear` defaults to 2040 (or the last gap year, 2039, if the gap ends immediately before). If the last maturity is before 2040, that year is used. The dropdown includes all available TIPS years to allow extension.

### RefCPI Date Override

By default, RefCPI is looked up for the settlement date. The UI allows overriding the reference date (click the RefCPI value in the info strip). When overridden:
- All P+I, cost, and ARA calculations use the RefCPI for the selected date instead of the settlement date
- Auto-inferred DARA (if previously computed) is cleared â€” rerun to refresh
- The info strip shows the override date in grey next to the RefCPI value

### DARA

DARA is entered by the user in the web UI (optional â€” leave blank to auto-infer with Full method).

#### Auto-Inference (Full method)

`inferDARAFromCash` binary-searches for the **largest integer DARA** at which `costDeltaSum â‰¥ 0` â€” i.e., the highest DARA the current portfolio can fund without buying any additional bonds.

#### Why Inferred DARA Can Exceed the Build DARA

When you build a ladder with DARA = $D, each rung quantity is computed as `round((D âˆ’ LMI) / PI_per_bond)`. Rounding sometimes allocates one more bond than strictly required (e.g., 24.51 â†’ 25). That fractional-bond surplus accumulates across all rungs. The binary search therefore finds a value slightly above $D â€” typically by $1â€“$100 â€” because the aggregate rounding surplus means every rung is still covered at the higher DARA without purchasing new bonds. The first DARA value that tips any rung over a rounding boundary and requires a new bond purchase is where the search stops.

Concretely: a Buildâ†’Rebalance symmetry test with explicit DARA = $40,000 produces `costDeltaSum = 0` exactly. Auto-inferring DARA from that same portfolio returns $40,049, because the built holdings over-fund by $49 due to rounding. At $40,050 the first rung needs one more bond and `costDeltaSum` goes negative.

**Consequence for symmetry:** Using auto-inferred DARA in Rebalance after a Build with explicit DARA produces a small excess-qty delta (typically Â±1 bond at a bracket year). This is the irreducible discrete residual of integer rounding â€” not a bug. For exact symmetry, supply the same explicit DARA to both Build and Rebalance.

#### Re-inference on Parameter Changes

If DARA was auto-inferred (not user-entered), changing any rebalance parameter (bracket mode, first/last year, PLI, RefCPI) and re-running triggers a fresh inference with the updated parameters. User-entered DARA is never overwritten.

### Pre-Ladder Interest (PLI) in Rebalance

When `preLadderInterest = true`, the rebalance zeroing pass mirrors the build-lib Â§3b logic:

1. **Prelim sweep** (longâ†’short): computes a funded-qty-based annual interest for each year in **`[firstYear, lastYear]` only**. Years outside this range (holdings to be sold above `lastYearOverride` or below `firstYearOverride`) are excluded â€” their interest must not inflate the pool.
2. `preLadderPool = preLadderYears Ã— totalAnnualInt` where `preLadderYears = firstYear âˆ’ settlementYear`.
3. **Distribution pass** (shortâ†’long from `firstYear` to `lastYear`): gap years consume pool credit first; funded years are zeroed when pool covers their need.

**Why scope matters**: if out-of-range holdings are included in the prelim sweep, `totalAnnualInt` is inflated, the pool becomes too large, too many funded years are zeroed, and their held-quantity â†’ 0 sell orders cause `costDeltaSum` to stay positive at unrealistically high DARA values during auto-inference.

### Per-Year DARA (`daraByYear`)

An optional `Map<fundedYear, number>` allowing each funded year to target a different annual real amount. When provided:

- Each year in Phase 4 uses `daraByYear.get(year) ?? DARA` as its target â€” years absent from the map fall back to the scalar DARA
- Gap years always use the scalar DARA (they have no TIPS and do not appear in the UI panel)
- Bracket target funded-year qty is computed with the bracket year's own DARA from the map
- When `daraByYear` is null/absent, behaviour is identical to the scalar-DARA path

## Named Quantities & Formulas (Rebalance)

**Excess Quantity Before** (`excessQtyBefore`):
```
excessQtyBefore_b = ROUND(totalBlockCost Ã— weight_b / costPerBond_b)
```
Cost-derived using current market prices â€” same formula as `excessQtyAfter`. Derived independently of current holdings and LMI. The funded-year before-quantity at a bracket year follows as: `fyQtyBefore = qtyBefore âˆ’ excessQtyBefore`.

**Excess Quantity After** (`excessQtyAfter`):
```
excessQtyAfter_b = ROUND(totalBlockCost Ã— weight_b / costPerBond_b)
```
Post-rebalance excess for duration matching. `costPerBond = price/100 Ã— indexRatio Ã— 1000` (per **TIPS Basics**).

**Exception â€” 3-bracket original lower bracket**: To avoid spurious trades due to cost precision rounding, `excessQtyAfter` is set directly to `excessQtyBefore` (freeze holdings).

**Cash Delta** (`cashDelta`, `costDelta`):
```
cashDelta = -(qtyDelta Ã— costPerTips)
  where qtyDelta = qtyAfter - qtyBefore
```
Positive = cash received (net sell); negative = cash outflow (net buy).

## Bracket Identification Rules

These rules apply when selecting which existing holdings serve as the lower bracket for gap coverage:
1.  **CUSIP Aggregation**: The total quantity of each CUSIP must be summed across the entire portfolio (all accounts and all maturity years).
2.  **Excess ARA Priority**: The primary identification metric is **Excess ARA** (`araByYear[y] - DARA`). The lower bracket is identified as the CUSIP in the year with the greatest positive difference between its current ARA and the target DARA. This ensures TIPS with the most "spare" capacity for duration matching are prioritized. **Note**: "Excess ARA" is a one-time empirical probe used solely here to locate the original lower bracket year. It is not a general concept â€” `araByYear[y]` is defined only for funded years (funded TIPS P+I + LMI from later bonds); excess holdings contribute to LMI flowing to shorter rungs but have no `araByYear` of their own.
3.  **Tie-Breaking**: Among candidates with equal Excess ARA, pick the single CUSIP with the highest aggregate quantity.

## Algorithm Flow (Gap-Only)

### Phase 1: Derive Parameters
- Settlement date, RefCPI, DARA.
- Build holdings ladder and identify official range.
- Calculate **inferred DARA** from current ARA.
- Identify current gap years.

### Phase 2: Missing Block Parameters
- Calculate **Gap Avg Duration** and **Gap Total Cost** (see **2.0 TIPS Ladders**).
- If `lastYear > 2056`, calculate **Future 30Y Avg Duration** and **Future 30Y Total Cost**.

### Phase 3: Bracket Resolution
- Identify lower/upper bracket CUSIPs from holdings per Â§Bracket Identification Rules.
- Calculate modified duration for each bracket.
- Solve weights using **2.0 TIPS Ladders** (2-bracket or 3-bracket mode).
- If `lastYear > 2056`, identify Future 30Y cover pair and solve weights.

### Phase 4: Ladder Rebuild (longest to shortest)
Process ALL years in [firstYear, lastYear] **longest to shortest**.
- **Bracket years**: use BEFORE-state LMI pool to ensure `fyQty` remains constant.
- **Rebalance years**: use running post-rebalance LMI pool.
- Apply bracket excess quantities on top of target `fyQty`.
- **LMI pool rule**: Process according to **2.0 TIPS Ladders Â§Algorithm**. Excess bracket holdings are actual bonds â€” their interest reduces funded-year quantities of all shorter rungs, on equal footing with funded-year holdings.

## Excess Balance Check

Validates historical funding of the gap coverage task:
```
1. Previous excess cost
2. Cost to fill rebalance years
3. Gap theoretical cost

Surplus/(Shortfall) = 1 - 2 - 3
```
Near-zero indicates brackets were properly funded. Large negative indicates brackets were underfunded relative to the current DARA target.
